Intro

Hi there, I'm Ethan Phan, a cloud software engineer. I grew up in the middle of a busy outskirt district in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.

I design and build data pipeline using AWS Services, CI/CD pipelines tools like Bitbucket, and have a huge passion in mathematics, ML/AI algorithms. I'm a certified AWS Solution Architect and AWS Developer Associate level and currently on my journey to obtain the AWS Certified DevOps Engineer - Professional. These certificates get me one step closer to Cloud Computing where I can connect my skills closer to building useful ML/AI tools.

I graduated from The University of Queensland with a Bachelor of Computer Science, Machine Learning major. It took me nearly 8 years of my 20s. Click on About me so I can tell you more about being a Late Bloomer is the greatest achievement in my 20s

Work

I'm currently working as a Cloud Software Engineer at TechConnect IT Solutions, Queensland, Australia. My daily responsibilities include researching, developing, deploying, and maintaining AWS cloud applications. I have strong experience with these AWS tools:

  • Compute: AWS Lambda, Amazon Elastic Compute Cloud (EC2)
  • Storage: S3
  • Networking and Content Delivery: VPC, Cloudfront, API Gateway
  • Database: Aurora, DynamoDB
  • Analytics: Kinesis, Athena, Quicksight, Glue
  • AWS Cost Management: AWS Budgets
  • Management & Governance: AWS Organisation, Cloudwatch, Cloudformation (IaC), Terraform
  • Security, Identity & Compliance: AWS Shield, AWS IAM
  • Container Service: Elastic Container Service (ECS), Elastic Container Registry (ECR)
  • Application Integration Services: Step Functions, EventBridge, SNS, SQS

A Late Bloomer

I restarted my University life when most my friends already started their PhD or Master.

My parents often said: "Being a software engineer won't get you anywhere, look at your all siblings being doctors at such early age!". I was often asked :"Is it too late to start your career in late 20s?"

I got my degree much later comparing to the majority, but it was the right time for me.

The experience I have had may not be similar to that crowd, but I know what it is like to be homeless, spit on, beaten up and to understand even if there's only 1% of hope, choose hope.

The life I'm having is a journey, not a race. There is neither late nor early.
